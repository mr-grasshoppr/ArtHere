Rosa Medina

Textile Artist
Alberta Arts District · Portland, OR

Rosa Medina weaves, dyes, and stitches her way through stories her grandmother taught her in Oaxaca. Her work spans wall-scale tapestries to small, wearable pieces, all rooted in natural dye traditions she has spent the past decade studying and adapting to Pacific Northwest plants.

Rosa came to Portland in 2017 and now keeps a studio above Alberta Street Gallery, where she also teaches occasional workshops in indigo dyeing and backstrap weaving. Her work has been featured in MAKE Magazine and shown at the Crafts Museum in Portland and in collaborative exhibitions with the Latino Network.

She is currently working on a series of textile maps that trace migration routes through color, gesture, and the geography of plants.
